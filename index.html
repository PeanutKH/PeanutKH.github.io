The Atomic Bomb - One of the deadliest weapons ever made - Uncover its history

The first atomic bomb ever produced was by the Manhattan Project. First started in December 1941, it was a research and development program led by the US in collaboration with Canada and the UK with the aim to develop nuclear weapons before Nazi Germany.

At its peak, the Manhattan Project employed nearly 130 thousand people, costing a modern equivalent of $27 Billion USD (in 2023). Over 80% of the cost came from building and operating plants that produced the fissile material, and more than 30 research sites were built across the US, Canada and the UK.
